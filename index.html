Nelson Mandela

Biography Tribute

Nelson Mandela was the first black president of South Africa, elected after being in prison for 27 years he got the Novel Peace Prize in 1993 for the effort to dismantle the country's aparheid system.
Mandela was born on 1918 in a small village of Mvezo in Transkei, South Africa with the birth name of Rolihlahla Mandela.
When he was 12 years old his father died of lung disease, and he was adopted by Chied Jongintaba, gesture done as a favor to Mandela's father, causing his life to change dramatically.
During a period he developed an interest in African history from elder chiefs from who learned how the African people had lived in relative peace until the coming of the white people.
In 1961, Mandela was arrested for leading a three-day national workers' strike and was sentenced to five years in prison. Two years later he was brought to trial again, and this time, he and other 10 ANC leaders were sentenced to life imprisionment for political offenses, including sabotage.
During his 27 years in prison he was able to earn a Bachelor of Law degree through a University of London and internationally, he continued to be such a potent symbol of black resistance. In 1990 with increasing local and international pressure for his release, the goverment participaed in several talks with Mandela but not deal was made. And it wasn't until Botha suffered a stroke and was replaced by Frederik Willem de Klerk that Mandela's release was finally announced.
In 1991, Mandela was elected president of the African National Congress, and in 1993 Mandela and President de klerk were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Price for their work toward dismantling apartheid in South Africa.
